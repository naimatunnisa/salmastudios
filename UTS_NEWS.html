Rum’s Identity Closer Than Ever?

The latest episode of Detective Conan has reignited one of the series’ longest-running mysteries: the true identity of Rum. Known as the right-hand man of the Black Organization, Rum has been shrouded in secrecy for over a decade. But in this episode, Conan and Haibara uncover a cryptic clue — a phrase and a gesture that seem oddly familiar — pointing toward someone long believed to be trustworthy.

Fans are buzzing with theories, dissecting every frame and line of dialogue. Could Rum be hiding in plain sight? Is this the beginning of the end for the Organization’s secrecy? With tension mounting and the stakes higher than ever, the series seems poised to deliver one of its most explosive revelations yet.

Conan Movie 28 to Spotlight Kaito Kid!

Spring 2026 will mark the release of Detective Conan’s 28th movie — and this time, the spotlight is on Kaito Kid. The charismatic thief will take center stage in a story set across Europe, where a legendary artifact has gone missing under suspicious circumstances. The teaser poster hints at gothic architecture, moonlit rooftops, and a chase that spans multiple countries.

Rumors suggest that Conan may be forced to team up with Kid to unravel a deeper conspiracy, possibly involving the FBI and a secret auction of stolen treasures. The dynamic between the two rivals — one a genius detective, the other a master illusionist — is expected to reach new heights. With international stakes and cinematic flair, this movie could redefine the Kid-Conan rivalry for a new generation.

Gosho Aoyama Confirms Conan’s Ending Is Ready

In a rare and candid interview, Gosho Aoyama — the legendary mangaka behind Detective Conan — confirmed that the series’ ending has been planned for years. “I’ve had the final scene in mind since the early days,” he said. While he didn’t reveal when the manga will conclude, he emphasized that readers who’ve followed closely may already sense how it all wraps up.

This revelation has sparked a wave of excitement and speculation among fans. Online forums are flooded with rereads, theory threads, and timeline breakdowns. Some believe the final confrontation with the Black Organization is imminent, while others suspect a more emotional twist involving Shinichi and Ran.

Aoyama’s statement adds a layer of urgency to every new chapter. With the pieces already on the board, the question now is: who will solve the final mystery first — Conan, or the fans?
